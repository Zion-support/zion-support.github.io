Titan Manufacturing Corporation, a global leader in precision components with $8B annual revenue and 45,000 employees across 80 facilities, faced mounting challenges from legacy systems, quality issues, and supply chain disruptions. ## Business Challenges

### 1. Operational Inefficiency
- **30-40%** machine downtime from unplanned maintenance
- **15%** overall equipment effectiveness (OEE) below industry average
- Manual, paper-based processes across production floors
- Disconnected systems preventing real-time visibility

### 2. Quality Issues
- **8.5%** defect rate causing rework and customer complaints
- Limited root cause analysis capabilities
- Reactive rather than preventive quality management
- $45M annual cost of poor quality

### 3. Supply Chain Disruptions
- Average **27 days** of raw material inventory
- Frequent stockouts and production delays
- Limited supplier visibility
- Inefficient logistics and warehousing

### 4. Legacy Systems
- 20+ year-old ERP and MES systems
- Limited integration between manufacturing and business systems
- Lack of real-time data for decision making
- High IT maintenance costs

### 5. Workforce Challenges
- Aging workforce with critical knowledge at risk
- Difficulty attracting and retaining talent
- Limited training capabilities
- Safety incidents averaging 3.2 per million hours
