## Technology Architecture

### Platform Components

**1. Document Ingestion**
- Multi-channel input (email, API, scanner, web portal)
- Automatic document classification using CNN
- Quality assessment and enhancement
- Duplicate detection and handling

**2. AI Processing Pipeline**
```
Document Receipt
    ↓
Pre-processing (deskew, denoise, enhance)
    ↓
OCR & Text Extraction (Tesseract 5.0 + Google Vision)
    ↓
Document Classification (DistilBERT)
    ↓
Entity Recognition & Extraction (Custom BERT models)
    ↓
Validation & Quality Checks
    ↓
Confidence Scoring & Routing
    ↓
• High confidence (>95%) → Straight-through processing
• Medium confidence (85-95%) → Automated review
• Low confidence (<85%) → Human review queue
```

**3. Integration Layer**
- Guidewire (Policy Administration)
- Duck Creek (Claims Management)
- Salesforce (Customer Portal)
- SharePoint (Document Management)
- Custom APIs for internal systems

**4. Analytics & Monitoring**
- Real-time processing dashboards
- Accuracy and quality metrics
- Business intelligence (Tableau)
- Alerting and notifications
- Continuous improvement tracking

### AI Models Deployed

**Computer Vision:**
- **Layout Analysis**: LayoutLM for document structure understanding
- **OCR**: Tesseract 5.0 + Google Vision API + custom models
- **Image Enhancement**: OpenCV-based preprocessing
- **Handwriting Recognition**: Custom CNN for cursive/print

**Natural Language Processing:**
- **Classification**: DistilBERT fine-tuned on insurance documents
- **Named Entity Recognition**: Custom BERT models for insurance entities
- **Key-Value Extraction**: Transformer-based extraction models
- **Semantic Understanding**: GPT-4 for complex document interpretation

**Validation & Quality:**
- **Business Rules Engine**: 500+ validation rules
- **Cross-Field Validation**: Graph neural networks for consistency
- **Fraud Detection**: Anomaly detection using isolation forests
- **Confidence Scoring**: Ensemble models for quality assurance

## Lessons Learned & Best Practices

### Critical Success Factors

**1. Start with High-Volume Standardized Documents**
- Medical claims perfect pilot (high volume, consistent format)
- Quick wins built momentum and buy-in
- Learning applied to more complex documents

**2. Invest in Data Quality**
- 50,000 historical documents labeled for training
- Continuous data collection and refinement
- Quality data → quality models

**3. Human-in-the-Loop Design**
- Smart escalation for uncertain documents
- User corrections feed back into model training
- Continuous improvement (98.4% → 99.2% accuracy)

**4. Focus on Integration**
- Seamless workflow with existing systems critical
- API-first architecture enabled rapid integration
- Real-time processing required real-time data exchange

**5. Change Management is Critical**
- Transparent communication about workforce impact
- Retraining and redeployment prevented layoffs
- User adoption key to success

### What We'd Do Differently

**1. Faster Pilot**
- Could have moved faster on pilot (took 8 weeks, could do in 4)
- Earlier demonstration of ROI would have accelerated buy-in

**2. More Training Data Initially**
- Started with 50K documents, should have collected 100K
- Would have reached target accuracy faster

**3. Earlier Stakeholder Engagement**
- Regulatory and compliance teams should have been involved from day 1
- Would have avoided some rework on audit requirements
