Trusted AI requires disciplined data governance. This guide covers the core pillars — privacy, security, lineage, policy enforcement, and compliance — and provides a pragmatic blueprint for enterprises building AI systems at scale.

## Governance Pillars

- Privacy-by-design with PII classification, masking, and purpose-based access
- Defense-in-depth: encryption in transit/at rest, key rotation, zero trust
- Lineage across data pipelines, features, and models for traceability
- Policy-aware inference with RBAC/ABAC and full auditability
- Compliance automation (SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, GDPR, AI Act readiness)

## Reference Architecture

1. Central policy engine mediating access and approvals
2. Metadata catalog + lineage graph linked to features and model artifacts
3. Observability for drift, bias, performance, and incident response

## Outcomes

- Faster approvals with continuous control evidence
- Lower incident impact with rapid root-cause analysis
- Safer collaboration via governed data products and contracts
