## Quantum Computing Fundamentals

### Quantum Bits (Qubits)
Unlike classical bits that exist in states of 0 or 1, qubits can exist in superposition, allowing them to represent multiple states simultaneously. This property enables quantum computers to process vast amounts of information in parallel.

### Quantum Gates and Circuits
Quantum gates manipulate qubit states, creating quantum circuits that perform computations. The most powerful quantum algorithms leverage:
- **Superposition**: Multiple states simultaneously
- **Entanglement**: Correlated quantum states
- **Interference**: Wave function manipulation

### Quantum Advantage
Quantum computers excel at problems involving:
- **Combinatorial optimization**
- **Machine learning on large datasets**
- **Cryptographic analysis**
- **Molecular simulation**
