## The Neural Interface Revolution: Understanding the Technology

### What Are Neural Interfaces?

Neural interfaces are devices that create direct communication pathways between the human brain and external computer systems. These interfaces can:

- **Read neural signals** from the brain
- **Transmit information** directly to neural pathways
- **Enhance cognitive abilities** through neural stimulation
- **Enable thought-controlled** business operations

### Types of Neural Interfaces

#### Non-Invasive Interfaces (2026-2027)
- **EEG-based systems** for brainwave reading
- **fNIRS technology** for neural activity monitoring
- **Transcranial stimulation** for cognitive enhancement
- **Wearable neural** devices for continuous monitoring

#### Semi-Invasive Interfaces (2027-2028)
- **Intracortical electrodes** for precise neural recording
- **Optogenetics** for neural pathway control
- **Neural dust** sensors for distributed monitoring
- **Flexible neural** implants for long-term use

#### Fully Invasive Interfaces (2028-2030)
- **Neural lace** integration with brain tissue
- **Synthetic neural** pathway creation
- **Direct memory** access and enhancement
