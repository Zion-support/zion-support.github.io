Enterprises need a pragmatic path to govern AI systems as they scale. This guide presents a maturity model with clear capabilities, signals, and outcomes.

## Levels overview

1. Ad hoc: Manual controls, limited visibility
2. Basic: Documented policies, partial reviews
3. Managed: Policy-as-code checks in CI/CD, incident runbooks
4. Advanced: Live eval gates, evidence bundles, centralized dashboards
5. Optimized: Continuous improvement loops, risk-based rollouts, signed attestations

## Key capabilities by level

- Policy-as-code coverage
- Risk register and ownership
- Evaluation harnesses and live gates
- Telemetry and audit evidence
- Exception workflows and approvals

## Metrics that matter

- Time-to-detect and MTTR for AI incidents
- Guardrail block rate vs. false positives
- Eval coverage across critical paths
- Evidence completeness for audits

## Getting started

Pick two critical workflows, define risks and controls, wire policy checks, and pilot live evals on canary traffic. Measure, iterate, and scale.
