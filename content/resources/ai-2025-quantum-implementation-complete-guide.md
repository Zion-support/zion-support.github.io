## Quantum Computing Fundamentals

### What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum computing leverages quantum mechanical phenomena to process information in ways that classical computers cannot. Key concepts include:

**Qubits**: Quantum bits that can exist in multiple states simultaneously
**Superposition**: Ability to be in multiple states at once
**Entanglement**: Quantum correlation between particles
**Quantum Interference**: Wave-like behavior of quantum systems

### Quantum vs. Classical Computing

| Aspect | Classical Computing | Quantum Computing |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Data Representation** | Binary (0 or 1) | Quantum states (0, 1, or both) |
| **Processing** | Sequential | Parallel |
| **Problem Solving** | Deterministic | Probabilistic |
| **Scalability** | Linear | Exponential |
| **Error Rates** | Very low | Higher (improving rapidly) |

### Quantum Computing Types

**Gate-Based Quantum Computers**:
- Universal quantum computers
- Suitable for complex algorithms
- Examples: IBM, Google, IonQ

**Quantum Annealers**:
- Specialized for optimization problems
- More stable and commercially available
- Examples: D-Wave, Fujitsu

**Quantum Simulators**:
- Classical computers simulating quantum systems
- Useful for algorithm development
- Examples: Microsoft Azure Quantum, AWS Braket
