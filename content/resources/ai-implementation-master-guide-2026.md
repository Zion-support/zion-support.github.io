## ROI Measurement and Tracking

### ROI Calculation Methodology

#### Direct Cost Savings

**Labor Cost Reduction**:
```
Formula: (Hours Saved × Hourly Rate × Number of Users) × 12 months

Example:
- Hours Saved per User per Day: 4 hours
- Hourly Rate: $50
- Number of Users: 100
- Annual Savings: 4 × $50 × 100 × 250 = $5,000,000
```

**Process Efficiency Gains**:
```
Formula: (Time Reduction % × Process Cost) × Volume

Example:
- Time Reduction: 60%
- Process Cost: $100 per transaction
- Monthly Volume: 10,000 transactions
- Monthly Savings: 60% × $100 × 10,000 = $600,000
```

**Error Reduction Savings**:
```
Formula: (Error Rate Reduction × Error Cost) × Volume

Example:
- Error Rate Reduction: 90%
- Error Cost: $500 per error
- Monthly Errors: 1,000
- Monthly Savings: 90% × $500 × 1,000 = $450,000
```

#### Revenue Increase

**New Revenue Streams**:
```
Formula: New Revenue - Additional Costs

Example:
- New Revenue: $2,000,000 annually
- Additional Costs: $500,000 annually
- Net Revenue Increase: $1,500,000
```

**Customer Acquisition**:
```
Formula: (New Customers × Average Customer Value) - Acquisition Costs

Example:
- New Customers: 500 annually
- Average Customer Value: $10,000
- Acquisition Costs: $2,000,000
- Net Revenue: (500 × $10,000) - $2,000,000 = $3,000,000
```

#### Total ROI Calculation

```
ROI = (Total Benefits - Total Investment) / Total Investment × 100%

Where:
Total Benefits = Direct Savings + Revenue Increase + Indirect Benefits
Total Investment = Technology + Implementation + Training + Ongoing Costs

Example:
- Total Benefits: $15,000,000
- Total Investment: $3,000,000
- ROI = ($15,000,000 - $3,000,000) / $3,000,000 × 100% = 400%
```
