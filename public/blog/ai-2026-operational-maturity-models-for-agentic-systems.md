# Operational Maturity Models for Agentic Systems (2026)

Modern teams are moving from prototypes to production-grade autonomous agents. This guide outlines an evidence-first maturity model that helps you scale safely while maintaining speed.

## Why maturity models?

Maturity models provide shared language and milestones. They reduce debate and enable measurable progress.

## Levels

1. Ad hoc — No clear objectives, manual checks, minimal telemetry.
2. Managed — Baseline SLIs, incident tracking, rollback playbooks.
3. Defined — Eval gates, policy events, and standard runbooks.
4. Quantified — Scorecards drive rollouts, budgets, and guardrails.
5. Optimizing — Continuous experiments with cost/quality/latency tradeoffs.

## Key artifacts

- SLIs and SLOs aligned to outcomes
- Evaluation gates and offline/online test suites
- Policy event logging and approvals
- Incident runbooks and rollback automation
- Evidence bundles for compliance and reviews

## Getting started

Pick 3 SLIs, wire a single eval gate, and create a rollback runbook. Iterate weekly with scorecards.
