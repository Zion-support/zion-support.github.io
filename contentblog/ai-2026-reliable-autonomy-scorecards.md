Shipping reliable autonomy at scale requires crisp objectives, measurable signals, and fast feedback loops. In this guide, we introduce a pragmatic scorecard covering safety, quality, reliability, and cost so platform teams can make consistent go/no-go decisions.

## Why scorecards now
- Regulatory pressure and customer trust demand audit-ready evidence
- Multi-model, multi-agent stacks add compounding failure modes
- Budgets and latency constraints require cost-aware trade-offs

## The core dimensions
1. Safety: violation rate, override rate, policy coverage
2. Quality: groundedness, answer accuracy, helpfulness
3. Reliability: task success, MTTR, tail latency, availability
4. Cost: tokens, runtime, tool calls, infra utilization

## Adoption checklist
- Define SLIs and SLOs per capability
- Gate releases via eval suites and policy-as-code
- Track incidents with root-cause evidence
- Publish weekly scorecards to stakeholders

Adopt this scorecard to align teams, reduce risk, and accelerate iteration while preserving trust.
