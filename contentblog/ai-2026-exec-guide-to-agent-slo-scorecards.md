Executive leaders need a crisp view of how autonomous systems perform — not just anecdotes.

This guide summarizes the minimum viable scorecards for agent platforms, translating engineering telemetry into business‑relevant metrics:

- Reliability SLOs: task success rate, incident rate, MTTR
- Safety SLOs: override rate, violation rate, containment effectiveness
- Quality SLOs: groundedness, hallucination containment, evaluation pass rate
- Efficiency SLOs: cost per completed task, latency SLIs

Recommended actions:

1. Adopt a tiered SLO set aligned to risk and impact
2. Wire eval gates to releases and incident workflows
3. Instrument traces with task IDs and approval contexts
4. Review weekly exec scorecards with clear “ship/hold” guidance

Outcome: faster decisions, safer autonomy, and measurable ROI.

## What leaders need

Executives need a single lens on reliability, safety, and value. SLO scorecards turn technical signals into business‑aligned commitments and red/green health.

### Scorecard ingredients

- Reliability: successful task completion, rollback rate, MTTR
- Safety: override rate, violation rate, tool misuse incidents
- Quality: groundedness, factuality, user satisfaction proxies
- Efficiency: latency budgets, cost per task, cache hit rate

### Operating with SLOs

- Gate releases on score thresholds and trending
- Tie incidents to SLO breaches with clear ownership
- Publish weekly deltas and corrective actions

Outcome: faster iteration with shared truth between engineers and executives.
