## Why AI Ops now

Organizations are moving from copilots to autonomous workflows. In 2025, AI Ops focuses on measurable business impact, safety, and reliability—not just demos. This guide outlines production patterns that work.

### Key pillars

- Objectives and guardrails defined up-front
- Evaluation-driven development and release health
- Human-in-the-loop escalation and override
- Cost, latency, and quality budgets enforced continuously

## Reference architecture

1. Event sources → streaming bus
2. Orchestrator (state machine) → tool-using agents
3. Policy and guardrails (PII, security, rate limits)
4. Evaluators (unit, offline, online)
5. Observability (traces, prompts, outcomes)

## Rollout playbook

1. Start with a narrow, high-ROI workflow
2. Define KPIs, constraints, and failure modes
3. Ship canary with strict budgets and alerts
4. Expand coverage after hitting reliability SLOs

## Results to target

- 20–40% cycle time reduction on targeted workflows
- 10–25% cost efficiency via automated rightsizing
- Fewer incidents through self-healing runbooks
